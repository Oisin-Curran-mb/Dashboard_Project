## What This Widget Shows Today

Per `Step 1 - Dashboard Research/15 - Bank Balances.md`: an **All Accounts view** (default) — table of active bank accounts + ending balances, a pie chart of balance distribution, totals row — and a **Single Account view** when one account is selected, switching entirely to a fixed 7-row breakdown (Beginning Balance, Deposits, Voids, Checks, Withdrawals, EFT, Ending Balance) plus a bar chart of 4 activity types. Balances reflect unreconciled transactions only, added to the last reconciliation's ending balance. **Note:** already Step 4 — locked (`Step 4 - Widget Final Design/W15 - Bank Balances.md`) — All Accounts gets Table/Bar/Cards as peer views; the Single Account breakdown is kept as-is, explicitly flagged in the design doc as "a legacy-specific feature with no direct competitor confirmation either way." Flagged ⚠️ high-risk per the punch list.

## Data Used

`BR_BankAccount`, `BR_Item` (typed transactions, unreconciled only), `BR_Reconcile` (per Step 1). Per `Step 3 - Mock_Work/Data and Build Readiness - Developer Punch List.md`'s `## W15` section: Account filter ✅ available; All Accounts Table/Bar/Cards views ✅ available; KPI Total Balance ✅ available; **Single Account mode — 7-row breakdown + bar chart 🔴 major gap** — the Modern API's single-account endpoint returns only a summary balance, with no breakdown or activity-chart endpoint implemented at all, arguably the more valuable of the widget's two modes per its own Purpose statement; reconciliation status display 🔴 open question, not decided either way.

## Competitor / Industry Findings

**Multi-account overview: one tile/card per account is the dominant pattern; no competitor uses a pie/donut for cross-account comparison.**
QuickBooks Online's Banking tab shows one tile per connected account (bank balance, book balance, transactions-to-review count) — card-based, not chart-based. *Sources: [Connect bank and credit card accounts to QuickBooks Online](https://quickbooks.intuit.com/learn-support/en-us/help-article/banking/connect-bank-credit-card-accounts-quickbooks/L4yDAHMNH_US_en_US) (updated ~Jul 2026); [Bank/credit card subaccount setup](https://quickbooks.intuit.com/learn-support/en-us/help-article/bank-connectivity/bank-credit-card-subaccount-setup/L57BbXXg8_US_en_US).* Xero mirrors this — each connected account gets its own dashboard widget (statement balance, Xero balance, unreconciled-lines count), up to 50 user-ordered widgets, no combined total and no cross-account chart. *Sources: [Xero — Analytics](https://www.xero.com/us/accounting-software/analytics/) (2026).* Mercury's "All Accounts" view is a list/table with a combined balance figure shown separately, not a chart. *Source: [Mercury — Viewing all Mercury accounts](https://support.mercury.com/hc/en-us/articles/35624262501524-Viewing-all-Mercury-accounts).* Ramp has no native aggregated multi-account balance dashboard at all — a user explicitly requested one in Oct 2025, confirming the gap rather than filling it. *Source: [Ramp Community — Aggregated Dashboard for bank accounts](https://community.ramp.com/t/aggregated-dashboard-for-bank-accounts/1185).* **Confidence: confirmed pattern (2+ sources)** on cards/tiles being dominant; **single-source negative finding** — no pie/donut for cross-account comparison was found anywhere, including in [Coupler.io's financial dashboard guide](https://blog.coupler.io/financial-data-visualization/), which uses pie charts for vendor breakdowns but plain KPI line items for account balances.

**Single-account drill-in: mainstream platforms default to an interactive, filterable ledger, not a fixed summary — and no direct precedent exists for this widget's specific fixed 4-category bar chart.**
QuickBooks Online's account register is a live, sortable, filterable ledger (by amount/reference/memo/date/type) — no bar chart of activity types. *Source: [Find, review, and edit transactions in account registers in QBO](https://quickbooks.intuit.com/learn-support/en-us/help-article/bank-registers/find-review-edit-transactions-account-registers/L2zTRtQRZ_US_en_US).* The closest structural analog to this widget's fixed 7-row table is QuickBooks' and Xero's separate **reconciliation summary report** (Beginning Balance → Cleared Transactions → Cleared Balance → Uncleared Transactions → Register Balance) — but that's a distinct report, never merged into the primary drill-in view the way this widget does. *Sources: [QBO — View, print, or export a reconciliation report](https://quickbooks.intuit.com/learn-support/en-us/help-article/export-reports/view-print-export-reconciliation-report/L1yNaVaOG_US_en_US); [XO Report — Bank Summary](https://xo-report.com/docs/reports/bank-summary) (third-party Xero tool documenting the same Xero report fields).* Mercury's closest analog — a "money movement breakdown" chart of top inflow/outflow sources — is explicitly dynamic, updating with whatever filters are applied, never fixed to a set list of categories. *Source: [Mercury — Getting the most out of our updated Transactions page](https://mercury.com/blog/updated-transactions-page) (2025-07-29).* **Confidence: confirmed pattern (3 platforms)** that a filterable ledger is the default drill-in; **zero sources found** for a fixed, non-filterable bar chart of exactly Deposits/Voids/Checks/Withdrawals — this remains a legacy-specific feature with no competitor validation either way, consistent with the existing Step 3/4 read.

**Reconciliation status: kept off the balance view in most platforms, with one real exception.**
QuickBooks Online, Ramp, and Mercury all keep reconciliation status entirely separate from the balance/dashboard view — QBO's reconciliation history sits under a distinct Reconcile menu, Ramp's unmatched-transaction count only appears inside its own Reconciliation Report, and Mercury has no reconciliation feature at all (as a bank, it happens downstream in the connected accounting tool). *Sources: [Ramp — QuickBooks Online integration: Reconciliation report](https://support.ramp.com); [Mercury Support](https://support.mercury.com).* **Confidence: confirmed pattern (3 independent platforms).** Xero is the one exception — a small numeric badge on the account tile shows the count of unreconciled/outstanding items, reading 0 when fully reconciled. **Confidence: single source, lower confidence** — this was corroborated at the snippet level via Xero Central community threads, but the primary Xero Central article itself is JS-rendered and could not be directly fetched to fully verify.

**Cash runway / days-of-cash: the advisory framing is well-supported, and real shipped UI examples exist — but fewer than a casual read might suggest.**
WhippleWood CPAs names "Days Cash on Hand" as one of the two most critical nonprofit board survival metrics, formula = Unrestricted Cash ÷ (Annual Cash Operating Expenses / 365), with a roughly 3-month benchmark. *Source: [WhippleWood CPAs — Eight Financial Ratios Every Nonprofit Board Should Review](https://whipplewood.com/insights/nonprofit-financial-ratios/) (2026-04-14, updated 2026-05-19).* Mercury's own Insights page implements Runway as a real, shipped UI metric — available balance ÷ monthly burn rate, suppressed when cashflow is positive. *Source: [Mercury — Insights page overview](https://support.mercury.com/hc/en-us/articles/44277089544084).* Geckoboard ships a dedicated "Cash Runway" KPI template. *Source: [Geckoboard — Cash Runway](https://www.geckoboard.com/resources/kpi-examples/cash-runway/).* **Confidence: confirmed pattern (2+ sources)** for both the advisory framing and real UI implementation. **Important correction from the prior research pass:** MIP Fund Accounting's own dashboard marketing makes no mention of "days cash on hand" or "runway" anywhere — no evidence of a runway metric there. *Source: [MIP — Meet MIP Dashboards](https://www.mip.com).* Sage Intacct's own blog on nonprofit dashboards discusses monitoring cash levels and reserve health but never uses "days cash on hand" or "runway" terminology in the primary text — a runway claim for Sage Intacct should be treated as **unconfirmed**, not corroborating evidence, correcting an overstatement in the prior version of this research. *Source: [Sage — Dashboards for nonprofits](https://www.sage.com/en-gb/blog/dashboards-for-nonprofits/) (2025-11-12).*

## Visual Options (aim for 3)

1. **Add a combined-total callout on the All Accounts view**, above or beside the account table, matching Mercury's pattern of leading with one aggregate figure. Based on: the Mercury finding above. Data needed: ✅ available today — the total is already computed for the existing totals row; this is a display change, not a new query.
2. **Add a cash runway / days-of-cash indicator**, computed from available balance and recent transaction burn rate. Based on: the WhippleWood/Mercury/Geckoboard findings — now confirmed by real shipped UI (Mercury, Geckoboard), not just advisory framing. Data needed: 🟡 needs new query/logic — the raw transaction data exists in `BR_Item`, but no burn-rate or runway calculation is defined anywhere in this project's current data/API docs; would need to be scoped and built.
3. **Consider a lightweight unreconciled-item count**, mirroring Xero's badge pattern, as a middle ground between the current no-indicator design and a full reconciliation-status display. Based on: the Xero finding (single source, lower confidence — the more common pattern across QBO/Ramp/Mercury is to keep this off the balance view entirely). Data needed: ✅ available today — the count is a straightforward `COUNT(BR_Item WHERE ReconcileID = null)`, already the basis of the widget's own balance calculation.

## Net Assessment

**Mixed — the core two-view structure is broadly consistent with how competitors split this problem, with one clarified gap and one meaningfully corrected finding.** The All Accounts pie chart still has no positive precedent anywhere researched — worth a second look, not because it's wrong, but because nothing validates it either, and cards/tiles are the clear market default. The Single Account fixed breakdown remains exactly what Step 3/4 already said: a legacy-specific feature with zero competitor validation either way, now confirmed by a fresh, deeper search rather than assumed. The most consequential change from the prior research pass is on cash runway: the advisory case and Mercury/Geckoboard's real implementations hold up, but MIP shows no evidence of a runway metric and the Sage Intacct claim was overstated before — it's unconfirmed, not corroborating. Reconciliation status is now backed by three independent platforms all keeping it off the balance view, with Xero's badge as the one lighter-weight alternative worth considering rather than a full status field.
